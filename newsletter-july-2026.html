I · Politics
Inside The Most Expensive Primary in US History

A 14-year incumbent, unseated in his own party's primary — after more money poured into one House race than most Senate campaigns see in a cycle. Joven follows the spending sector by sector to show exactly whose dollars did the talking, and what the hidden machinery of American democracy looks like when it decides a seat is worth buying.

II · Business
Why AI Will Not Replace Accountants

The Big Four are pouring billions into AI — and none of it is designed to remove the accountant. Sidhant looks inside platforms like KPMG Clara and Deloitte Omnia to make the contrarian case: the profession everyone says automation will kill is quietly becoming the one it can't function without.

By Sidhant Matthews  ·  Read the essay →
  
 
III · Politics
Ottawa's $1.45 Billion Condo Gambit

The federal government wants to buy 2,200 BC condos at a discount developers have little reason to offer. Kasra traces how BC housing starts collapsed nearly 80% from their peak, why a population whiplash left sellers and buyers in a standoff, and whether "let the prices drop" is a housing policy or just a good one-liner.

By Kasra Kazemialiakbar  ·  Read the essay →
  
 
IV · Business
Why Shoppers Choose Walmart, Costco, and Home Depot: The $1.12 Trillion Gold Standard in Retail

Together these three out-earn the GDP of Switzerland. Costco caps its markups at 14% when retail routinely runs 50–100%, Walmart draws 280 million visits a week, and 92% of Costco members keep coming back. Joven argues the secret was never a growth hack — it's Sam Walton's old rule that the customer can fire everybody in the company.
